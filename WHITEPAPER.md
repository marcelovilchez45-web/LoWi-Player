# High-Performance Audio Engine: Technical Whitepaper
**Author:** CTO & Lead Systems Architect
**Focus:** Bit-Perfect Architecture, DSP Precision, and Low-Latency Network Synthesis

## 1. Bit-Perfect Architecture: Bypassing AudioFlinger
In standard Android environments, the audio subsystem (AudioFlinger) forces resampling of all signals to a fixed rate (usually 48kHz) and bit depth (16-bit) to mix alerts and system sounds. This introduces quantization noise and aliasing.

Our engine utilizes the **AAudio API** in `Exclusive Mode` (built on Oboe). By requesting a direct path to the MMAP-capable HAL (Hardware Abstraction Layer), we bypass the system mixer entirely. 
- **Direct Path:** When an external USB DAC is detected via our custom `USB Host API` bridge, we open a raw communication channel.
- **Clock Mastery:** The C++ engine synchronizes its processing buffer precisely with the DAC's hardware clock, ensuring that not a single bit is altered or resampled.

## 2. DSP Architecture: 32-bit Floating Point & Biquad Synthesis
While standard equalizers often operate in the integer domain (causing rounding errors), our DSP pipeline uses **32-bit Floating Point (IEEE 754)** precision.
- **Linear Phase Considerations:** The 10-band parametric EQ uses second-order IIR Biquad filters. Each band is calculated in the `onAudioReady` high-priority callback.
- **Biquad Coefficients:** Coefficients are computed using the Bristow-Johnson formulas, optimized for stability in real-time.
- **Headroom Management:** A soft-knee clipper (sigmoid-based) is implemented at the final stage of the pipeline to prevent digital clipping while maintaining transient response.

## 3. Network Audio Bridge: LPCM over HTTP Synthesis
The "PC Link" feature bridges the mobile device and a desktop workstation.
- **Protocol Selection:** We chose LPCM (Linear PCM) over HTTP for its deterministic delivery and compatibility. Unlike lossy WebRTC implementations, LPCM ensures that every sample generated by the DSP is transmitted.
- **Jitter Mitigation:** On the receiver side (Python/receiver), we implement a **Ring Buffer (Jitter Buffer)**. This buffer dynamically scales to absorb Wi-Fi packet fluctuations while maintaining the lowest possible perceptual latency.
- **mDNS Discovery:** Utilizing Zeroconf, the mobile "Engine Host" announces its availability, allowing the receiver to establish a handshake without user-provided IP configurations.

## 4. Stability and SRE (Site Reliability Engineering)
To handle the volatility of the Android NDK environment:
- **Watchdog Timer:** A native monitor detects if the hardware callback stalls and restarts the stream within 50ms.
- **Signal Shield:** Custom handlers for `SIGSEGV` and `SIGFPE` capture stack traces from the audio thread, allowing for asynchronous debugging of low-level DSP failures.
